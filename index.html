Why reviews stopped meaning anything

A rating today can come from a bot, a rival, or someone who never ordered a thing.

Restaurants live and die by a number they can't control — one a competitor can dent or a stranger can inflate. Diners scroll past hundreds of reviews with no way to know which came from a real meal.

The problem was never too few reviews. It's that none of them had to be earned.

Dined In changes the rule: no visit, no review.

How verification works

Three ways to prove you were there.

It happens right at the table, and takes about as long as reading this sentence.

i

Scan the table code

Every Dined In restaurant places a code on the table. One scan confirms your visit — the simplest, strongest proof there is.

ii

Snap your receipt

No code at hand? Photograph your receipt. We match it to the restaurant and date, and the review is verified.

iii

Check in on location

Still at the table? A quick location check-in confirms you're really there before your review counts.
